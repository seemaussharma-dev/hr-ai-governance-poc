# 00 — Business Problem

## Organization

ssp-dev-hr-aigovern-poc is a fictional small broadcast and digital-media company with 75 employees in the United States and India.

## Current problem

HR receives repetitive questions about:

- vacation entitlement;
- leave-request timing;
- remote-work requests;
- expense-report deadlines;
- expense approvals.

Information is available in policy documents, but employees may not know where to look. HR answers the same questions repeatedly, and inconsistent answers can create confusion and risk.

## Why an automated assistant is considered

A policy assistant could provide fast, consistent access to approved policy information while keeping sensitive and individual matters with HR.

## Risks introduced by the solution

An HR assistant can create harm if it:

- collects personal data unnecessarily;
- exposes salary, medical, performance, complaint, or disciplinary records;
- invents company policy;
- makes or influences employment decisions;
- uses obsolete documents;
- fails to explain that it is automated;
- stores employee questions unnecessarily;
- has no human escalation path.

## Business objective

Demonstrate that a small company can design an HR policy assistant with governance controls from the beginning.

## MVP success statement

The MVP succeeds when it can answer approved general policy questions, block prohibited requests, escalate unsupported questions, cite its source, avoid raw-question logging, and produce evidence that its controls were tested.
